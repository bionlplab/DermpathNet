Dermoscopic Findings of Clonal Seborrheic Keratosis and Pigmented Basal Cell Carcinoma in the Skin of Color Patients - A Comparison.

Histopathologic examination showed (a) acanthotic epidermis with nodular collections of basaloid cells in the dermis containing aggregates of brownish-black pigment deposits. Keratin-filled horn cysts and melanophages were seen interspersed between the interwoven cords of basaloid, pale staining cells without any atypical features. (b) epidermis with multiple lobules composed of basaloid cells with scanty cytoplasm and hyperchromatic nuclei projecting downwards to the papillary dermis. Lobules contain pigment deposits and show peripheral palisading and artefactual retraction spaces between the lobules and surrounding fibro collagenous stroma. Stroma also contains pigment laden macrophages (hematoxylin-eosin; original magnification Ã— 10)
