The contribution of human papilloma virus infection to cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma in patients with chronic lymphocytic leukemia.

Histomorphologic characterization of cSCC lesions found in CLL patients. (A) Cutaneous SCC with markedly atypical malignant cells invading the deep dermis characteristic of conventional SCC. (H&E composite, 2x objective magnification [obj. mag.]; inset 40x). (B) SCC with papillomatosis, hyperkeratosis, focal parakeratosis, and focal hypergranulosis––consistent with wart‐like features (cSCC‐wf) H&E composite, 4x obj. mag. (C and D) Dermal lymphocytic infiltration seen in cSCC lesions of CLL patients: cSCC tumor ("T") with peritumoral small uniform lymphocytes with mild to moderate atypia suggestive of coincident CLL/SLL (arrows), (C: H&E, 20x obj. mag. Tumor‐adjacent early germinal center formation (D: H&E, 10x obj. mag; inset: H&E, 40x obj. mag.)
