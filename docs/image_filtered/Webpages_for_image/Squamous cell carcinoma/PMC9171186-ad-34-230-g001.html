A Case of Smooth Muscle Actin-Positive Cutaneous Spindle Cell Squamous Cell Carcinoma.

(A) A solitary nodule with central ulceration on the right preauricular region of the cheek. (B) Dermoscopic findings of central ulceration with scales, crusts and blood spots surrounded by arborizing vessels and adjacent whitish veil appearance. (C) Scanning view of the punch biopsy sample displaying ulcer, epidermal splitting, solar elastosis, and pan-dermal infiltration of atypical cells (H&E, ×40). (D) Atypical keratinocytes and spindle cells within the upper dermis and accompanying solar elastosis (H&E, ×100). (E, F) Proliferation of the atypical spindle cells in the deep dermis (H&E, ×100, ×200). Atypical spindle cells were positive for immunohistochemical stainings of (G) vimentin (×200), (H) SMA (×200), and (I) pan-cytokeratin (×200). (J) The preoperative lesion displayed the spreading of an ill-demarcated firm plaque connected with the main lesion (inner purple line [red arrow]). (K) The surgically excised specimen showed irregular keratinocytes proliferating downward and atypical spindle cells in the upper dermis accompanying epidermal connection (H&E, ×100). (L) It was a poorly differentiated SCC (H&E, ×200). (M) Invasion of tumor cells into the deep margin of the surgically excised specimen identified by pan-cytokeratin staining (×100). We received the patient’s consent form about publishing all photographic materials.

PMID: 35721338

Type: Premalignant/Malignant, Category: Keratinocytic
