Collision Carcinoma Involving Small Cell Neuroendocrine Carcinoma and Squamous Cell Carcinoma of the Ureter: A Case Report and Review of the Literature.-DermPath

Collision Carcinoma Involving Small Cell Neuroendocrine Carcinoma and Squamous Cell Carcinoma of the Ureter: A Case Report and Review of the Literature.

Immunohistochemical staining of some tumour cells showed that (A) cluster of differentiation 56 (CD56; magnification, x400) was positive, but (B) chromogranin A (CgA; magnification, x100) and (C) synaptophysin (Syn; magnification, x100) were negative. Positive immunohistochemical staining for (D) cytokeratin 5/6 (CK5/6; magnification, x400) and (E) regulator protein 63 (P63; magnification, x400). (F) The Ki-67 (magnification, x100) index of neuroendocrine tumour cells was 90%, while that of squamous cell tumours was 40%. The black dotted lines show the boundary between neuroendocrine tumours and squamous carcinomas.

PMID: 34290980

Type: Premalignant/Malignant, Category: Keratinocytic
