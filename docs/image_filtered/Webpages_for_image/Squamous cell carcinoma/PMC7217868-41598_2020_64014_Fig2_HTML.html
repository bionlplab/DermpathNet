Equine penile squamous cell carcinoma: expression of biomarker proteins and EcPV2.

Quantification of inflammatory cells on H&E stained tissue cores. A CNN, consisting of two convolution layers and a fully connected layer, was trained on a dataset of 1200 images (11 × 11 pixels) of inflammatory cells and 1897 images of non-inflammatory areas of tissue. Predictions were made on a 11 × 11 pixel regions of tissue (A–D; H&E stained tissue core image, (E–H); H&E stained tissue core image with predictions overlaid). Green squares represent non-inflammatory predictions and the larger red squares represent the centroid of an image that has been predicted as inflammation (E-H). The final value for inflammation was calculated as a fraction of predicted inflammatory areas over total areas of tissue for each core (EpSCC EcPV2 + n = 23, NT EcPV2 + n = 14, EpSCC EcPV2− n = 60, NT EcPV2− n = 17). Box plots (I, J) were constructed in GraphPad Prism to compare levels of CNN calculated inflammation in different pathologies (I) and EcPV2 infection status (J) (Mann Whitney U, ***p < 0.0001, **p < 0.001, *p < 0.05).
