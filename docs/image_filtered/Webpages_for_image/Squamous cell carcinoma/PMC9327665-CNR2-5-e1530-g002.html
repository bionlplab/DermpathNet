Smoking-induced radiation laryngeal necrosis after definitive radiotherapy alone for T1a glottic squamous cell carcinoma: A case report.

Hematoxylin–eosin‐stained sections (A) at ×100 magnification and (B) at ×200 magnification of the pharyngeal tumor at 13 months after radiotherapy. Significant inflammatory cell infiltration is observed. There was no recurrence of the primary cancer. Significant vitrification of blood vessel walls (arrow) and narrowing of the vascular lumen (arrow head) were observed. These typical findings presented ischemic change of the mucosa caused by late radiation toxicity

PMID: 34396712
