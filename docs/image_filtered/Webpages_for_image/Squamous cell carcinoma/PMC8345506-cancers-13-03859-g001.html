Superficially Invasive Vulvar Squamous Cell Carcinoma: A 37-Year-Long Experience of a Tertiary Referral Center.-DermPath

Superficially Invasive Vulvar Squamous Cell Carcinoma: A 37-Year-Long Experience of a Tertiary Referral Center.

Histological features of vulvar recurrence with groin metastasis. (a) Hematoxylin and eosin (H&E) slide showing an invasive squamous cell carcinoma consistent with vulvar superficially invasive cancer (original magnification: 20×). (b) Perineural tumor invasion was observed, while no lymphovascular invasion was noted (original magnification: 400×). (c,d) Double immunohistochemical staining for S100 (red) and cytokeratins (AE1/AE3) (brown) confirming perineural invasion (original magnification for c: 200×, for d: 400×). (e) p53 showing widespread positivity (original magnification: 40×). (f) ki-67 was mostly positive in the basal layer of the non-invasive component (original magnification: 40×).

PMID: 34359760

Type: Premalignant/Malignant, Category: Keratinocytic
