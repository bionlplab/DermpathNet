Initial-Stage Primary Intraosseous Squamous Cell Carcinoma Derived from Odontogenic Keratocyst with Unusual Keratoameloblastomatous Change of the Maxilla: A Case Report and Literature Discussion.

Microscopic features of the surgical specimen. (a) Invasion of atypical epithelium (black arrow). (b) Invasive squamous epithelium irregularly migrating from the epithelial cyst lining similar to SCC. (c) High-power magnification. (d) Invasive nests, (e) p53, and (f) Ki-67 (HE and IHC; original magnification: ×12.5 (a), ×40 (b), ×100 (c), and ×200 (d), (e), and (f)).
