Case Report: Management of rectal squamous cell carcinoma - a treatment dilemma.

Pathology of the rectal mass demonstrates a squamous carcinoma.At medium and high power magnification, hematoxylin and eosin (HE) staining reveals sheets of poorly differentiated squamous cells invading the surrounding submucosal tissue (HE x40 and x100). Immunohistochemical staining for the squamous cell marker CK5/6, visualized by a cytoplasmic red-chromogen reaction, is positive (CK5/6 x40 and x100). Together histopathology and immunostaining show a poorly differentiated squamous cell carcinoma originating from rectal tissue.

PMID: 32864106

Type: Premalignant/Malignant, Category: Keratinocytic
