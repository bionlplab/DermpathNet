Clinical Controversy Surrounding the Differential Diagnosis of Branchiogenic Carcinoma.

Histopathologic examination of the excised cervical lymph node revealed multiple cystic epithelial structures lined by stratified squamous epithelium exhibiting to a great extent malignant cytologic (hyperchromatic, irregular nuclei, mitotic figures) and architectural (loss of polarity) features (a); lying on dense lymphatic tissue focally forming lymphoid follicles with prominent germinal centers (b). The squamous cell epithelium lining of the cysts exhibited areas of transition (b, c) of moderate/severe dysplasia to in situ squamous cell carcinoma (d). A well-defined sinus tract was not present.
