Unusual recurrent tongue spindle cell carcinoma with marked anaplasia occurring at the site of glossectomy for a well-differentiated squamous cell carcinoma: A case report.

Biopsy specimen of the primary tumor. (A) Squamous cell carcinoma proliferation was present under the ulcerated lingual mucosa (magnification, ×40; H&E stain). (B) Cancer pearls were apparent in the tumor nests (magnification, ×100; H&E stain). H&E, hematoxylin and eosin.
