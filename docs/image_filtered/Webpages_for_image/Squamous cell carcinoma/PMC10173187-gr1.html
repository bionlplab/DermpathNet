Atypical mid rectal localization of squamous cell carcinoma: A case report and literature review.

Microscopy showing a rectal mucosa infiltrated by a carcinomatous process, tumor cells are arranged in nests and sheets pattern and show prominent nuclear atypia. This aspect is compatible with a moderately differentiated squamous cell carcinoma.A: Histologic examination showing a rectal mucosa infiltrated by a carcinomatous process (HE ×40).B: Tumor cells are arranged in nests and sheets pattern and showing a proeminent nuclear atypia (HE ×100).

PMID: 37119755

Type: Premalignant/Malignant, Category: Keratinocytic
