Squamous cell carcinoma of unknown primary origin in a dog presenting with bone metastasis.

Histopathologic findings in a dog with carcinoma of unknown primary. (A) Lung: Epithelial neoplastic proliferation shows high cellularity with poorly demarcated and infiltrative growth. These cells are organized in a tubular to papillary pattern, with marked anisokaryosis and discrete kariomegaly (Inset) (Hematoxylin and Eosin [H&E] stain, 100×; Inset, 400×; (B) Kidney: Neoplastic cells of epithelial origin, similar to that observed in the lung are seen. There is a predominant tubular distribution pattern, anisocytosis, anisokaryosis, and kariomegaly (Inset) [H&E stain, 100×; Inset, 400×]; (C and D) Lung: A strong immunohistochemistry expression of both AE1/AE3 (C) and 34BE12 (D) antibodies (brown color) is observed in the cytoplasm of the epithelial neoplastic cells (400×).

Type: Premalignant/Malignant, Category: Keratinocytic
