A Hybrid Epithelial to Mesenchymal Transition in Ex Vivo Cutaneous Squamous Cell Carcinoma Tissues.

Immunohistochemistry of ANT and cSCC tissues for pan-cytokeratin (red; Panels A–C) and vimentin (magenta; Panels D–F). At 40× magnification, cSCC images delineate cSCC (tumor) tissue from the dermal tissue and ANT images are oriented with the stratum corneum (sc) layer at the top. Images at 100× magnification show that poorly differentiated cells (poor) along the edges of the tumor have a higher (2–3+) expression of the mesenchymal marker vimentin (Panel F) compared to well-differentiated cells (well; 0–1+) in the center of the tumor mass. Poorly differentiated cells also show a visible reduction in the epithelial marker pan-cytokeratin (Panel C). Tissue sections were stained with H&E for validation of epithelial tissue (images not shown).

PMID: 36012449
