Intersecting Mechanisms of Hypoxia and Prostaglandin E2-Mediated Inflammation in the Comparative Biology of Oral Squamous Cell Carcinoma.

Areas of necrosis and new blood vessel formation in untreated human and feline OSCC. Human (A) and feline (B) OSCC tumours showing transition from intact, viable-appearing OSCC tumour cells (T) to areas of necrosis (*) characterised by loss of structure and replacement with eosinophilic (pink) proteinaceous material and cellular debris. Small blood vessels are present near the tumour cells (arrows). One of the causes of tumour necrosis is hypoxia. Within the stroma (S) of human (C) and feline (D) OSCC, numerous blood vessels are present (arrows), indicating growth of new blood vessels into the region. In both (C, D), inflammatory cells (neutrophils, arrowheads) are marginating along the endothelial surface. [haematoxylin and eosin stain, 20X objective (A, B) and 40X objective (C, D)].

PMID: 34094895
