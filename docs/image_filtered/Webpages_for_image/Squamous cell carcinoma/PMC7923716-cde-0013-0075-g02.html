Squamous Cell Carcinoma within a Congenital Nevus Sebaceous: Case Report and Literature Review.

Histopathologic findings demonstrate squamous cell carcinoma arising in the nevus sebaceous. a Focally keratinizing, atypical, squamous epithelial cells arising from the epidermis and extending into the dermis (H&E stain; original magnification, 2×). b The adjacent skin showed acanthosis and papillomatosis, with an abnormal location of sebaceous glands which opened directly to the skin surface, along with many ectopic apocrine glands associated with lacking anagen hair (H&E stain; original magnification, 4×). c Horn pearls and individual cell keratinization (H&E stain; original magnification, 10×). d Large eosinophilic tumor cells with focally glassy cytoplasm and vesicular nuclei (H&E stain; original magnification, 40×).

PMID: 33708087

Type: Premalignant/Malignant, Category: Keratinocytic
