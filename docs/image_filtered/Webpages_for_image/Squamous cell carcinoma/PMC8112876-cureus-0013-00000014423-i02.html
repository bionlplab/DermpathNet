Discordant Immune Marker Expression Between Preoperatively Biopsied and Matched Surgically Resected Specimens in Patients With Oral Squamous Cell Carcinoma.-DermPath

Discordant Immune Marker Expression Between Preoperatively Biopsied and Matched Surgically Resected Specimens in Patients With Oral Squamous Cell Carcinoma.

Biopsy (A-C) and surgically resected specimens (D-F) from a 72-year-old male patient with inferior gingival cancer in the stage of pT4aN0A and D, H&E staining (original magnification X400).B and E, Immunohistochemical analysis of PD-L1 expression (X400) showed approximately <1% tumor staining (nPD-L1 negative) in the biopsy specimen and 20% tumor staining (nPD-L1 positive) in the resection specimen.C and F, Immunohistochemical analysis of PD-1 expression (X400) showed lower number of PD-1-positive TILs (20 cells/HPF) in the biopsy specimen than the number of those (60 cells/HPF) in the resection specimen.H&E, Hematoxylin and eosin; nPD-L1, neoplastic PD-L1; TIL, tumor-infiltrating lymphocytes; HPF, high-power field.

PMID: 33996291

Type: Premalignant/Malignant, Category: Keratinocytic
