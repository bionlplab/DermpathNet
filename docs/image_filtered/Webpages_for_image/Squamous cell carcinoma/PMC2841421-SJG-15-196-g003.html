Squamous cell carcinoma of the rectum 21 years after radiotherapy for cervical carcinoma.

(a) (H and E, ×10) and E section of the rectal biopsy shows well differentiated squamous cell carcinoma with areas of keratinization that has become invasive. The typical columnar epithelium with goblet cells has been replaced with the neoplastic process into the submucosa; (b) (H and E, ×40) and E section of the rectal biopsy shows higher magnification of the neoplastic cells with large round nuclei with prominent nucleoli, along with the formation of keratin “pearls” seen in well differentiated squamous cell carcinoma

PMID: 19636183

Type: Premalignant/Malignant, Category: Keratinocytic
