Penile schwannoma mistaken for hemangioma: a rare case report and literature review.

Pathologic findings of the penile schwannoma. (A) Microscopic findings of excised specimen show the typical biphasic pattern composed of alternating Antoni A and Antoni B areas. [hematoxylin and eosin (H&E), ×40]. (B) Tumor cells show nuclear palisading with tapered ends interspersed with collagen fibers (H&E, ×200). (C) Immunohistochemical staining of S-100 show strong and diffuse expression in tumor cells (S-100 immunohistochemical staining ×200).

PMID: 34295737

Type: Benign, Category: Neural or neuroendocrine
