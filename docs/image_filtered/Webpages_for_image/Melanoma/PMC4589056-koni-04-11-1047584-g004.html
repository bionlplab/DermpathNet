Immunomodulatory effect of peritumorally administered interferon-beta on melanoma through tumor-associated macrophages.

CD163+ cells and CD206+ cells in the lesional skin of in-transit melanoma patients before and after IFN-β treatment. Paraffin-embedded tissue samples from the lesional skin of in-transit melanoma patients were deparaffinized and stained with anti-CD163 Ab or anti-CD206 Ab (A). Sections were developed with liquid permanent red (Original magnification ×100). Summary of the numbers of CD163+ cells and CD206+ cells in melanoma samples from five patients before and after the administration of IFN-β. Three representative fields of each section were selected from the dermis associated with dense dermal lymphoid infiltrate. The number of immunoreactive cells was counted by using an ocular grid of 1 cm2 at a magnification of ×400. The data are expressed as the mean and SD of the number of stained cells. * marks a significant (P < 0.05) difference.
