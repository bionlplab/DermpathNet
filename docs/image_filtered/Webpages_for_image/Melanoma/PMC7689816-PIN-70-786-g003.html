Surgically treated gastric melanoma of unknown primary: A case report from a 10-year survivor.

Microscopic findings of the tumors. (a) Neoplastic cells involved the muscularis propria to the submucosal layer under low‐power magnification (H&E). (b) Tumor cells grew in a sheet like fashion accompanied by scarce stroma (H&E, original magnification ×200). (c) The cells also invaded into the mucosa near the ulcer bed (H&E, original magnification ×100). (d) Individual cells showed irregular, enlarged nuclei containing rough chromatin and polygonal or spindle cytoplasm with high pleomorphism. Multinucleated cells could be observed at the center of the photograph (H&E, original magnification ×600). (e) Mitotic figures were often observed under high‐power magnification (H&E, original magnification ×400). (f) Many cells contained tiny tan granules in the cytoplasm, especially in the dark brown region (H&E, original magnification ×400).
