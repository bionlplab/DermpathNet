Exposition to Biological Control Agent Trichoderma stromaticum Increases the Development of Cancer in Mice Injected With Murine Melanoma.

Histopathological analysis of mice in experimental model. (A) Micrometastatic score from mice treated with conidia, B16-F10 and control; (B) in protocol 1 and (C) protocol 2. (D) Photomicrograph of lung from mice challenged only with conidia. (E) Representative photomicrograph of early pulmonary micrometastasis in the subpleural and (F) periarteriolar area of the lung parenchyma from mice challenged with conidia and B16-F10. Asterisk (*) indicate micrometastatic foci and black arrow (→) indicate the presence of melanin in micrometastatic foci. Paraformaldehyde-fixed, paraffin-embedded sections were stained with hematoxylin & eosin (H&E). The micrometastatic score was assessed by Mann-Whitney test. Data are presented as mean ± SEM (n = 5–6 mice per group). Value of p < 0.05 were considered for statistical significance.
