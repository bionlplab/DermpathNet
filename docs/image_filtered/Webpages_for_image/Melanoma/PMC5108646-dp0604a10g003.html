Early diagnosis of genital mucosal melanoma: how good are our dermoscopic criteria?

Histopathology of vulvar melanoma in situ. (a) Photomicrograph of initial biopsy specimen showing a broad, asymmetric, junctional melanocytic proliferation with variable epidermal acanthosis and dermal clusters of heavily pigmented melanophages (hematoxylin-eosin stain, original magnification ×100). (b, c, d) Images of excised specimen showing confluent, severely atypical melanocytes with pagetoid spread (b), hematoxylin-eosin stain, original magnification ×400), areas with lichenoid lymphoid infiltrates and melanophages (c), hematoxylin-eosin stain, original magnification ×200), and the intersection of congested and ectatic vasculature with dermal melanophages and junctional melanoma (d), hematoxylin-eosin stain, original magnification ×200).
