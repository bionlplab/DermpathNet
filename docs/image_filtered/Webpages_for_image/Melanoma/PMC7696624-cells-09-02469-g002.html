Tumor Necrosis Factor Receptor-1 (p55) Deficiency Attenuates Tumor Growth and Intratumoral Angiogenesis and Stimulates CD8(+) T Cell Function in Melanoma.

TNFR1 controls tumor proliferation in melanoma. Tumors in WT and TNFR1 KO mice were analyzed by immunohistochemistry to assess expression of proliferating cell nuclear antigen (PCNA), a marker of proliferating cells. (A) Hematoxylin & Eosin (H&E) and PCNA staining. The arrows on the H&E panels indicate mitotic cells, the arrows on the right panels indicate PCNA+ (top) and PCNA− cells (below). The images are representative of three independent experiments. Scale bar: 25 µm. (B) Quantification of PCNA+ cells. Data are mean ± SEM (n = 3). (C) Number of cell nuclei by field. Data are mean ± SEM (n = 3), ** p < 0.01.
