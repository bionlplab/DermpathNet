Significance of pigmented urothelial and non-urothelial cells in voided urine specimens: A case report and review.-DermPath

Significance of pigmented urothelial and non-urothelial cells in voided urine specimens: A case report and review.

(a) Highly cellular specimens showing numerous urothelial cells, acute inflammatory cells and pigmented cells (Pap stain ×100). (b) Numerous cells containing dark intracytoplasmic pigment obscuring the nucleus (Pap stain ×200). (c) Follow up urinary bladder biopsy showing metastatic malignant melanoma under a normal appearing mucosa (H and E stain ×200). (d) Bone marrow smear showing numerous, small plasmacytoid cells with brown intracytoplasmic pigment (H and E stain ×200)

PMID: 28469318

Type: Premalignant/Malignant, Category: Melanocytic
