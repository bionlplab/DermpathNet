Characteristics of Giant Nodular Melanomas in Special Locations: a Case Series and Review of the Literature.

a Dermal melanoma, epithelioid subtype. Breslow thickness and Clark level could not be assessed, high proliferation index Ki-67 80%, TILs nonbrisk (hematoxylin–eosin staining, magnification 50-fold). b Dermal melanoma. Positive staining for Melan-A, S-100, and HMB-45. Magnification 60-fold. c NM, epithelioid subtype, Breslow thickness 12 mm, Clark V, with the presence of ulceration and microsatellites (hematoxylin–eosin staining, magnification 50-fold). d NM, signs of neuro- and angio-invasion (hematoxylin–eosin staining, magnification 280-fold). e Deposits of melanin in NM biopsy (hematoxylin–eosin staining, magnification 130-fold). f NM with prominent nuclear inclusions (hematoxylin–eosin staining, magnification 380-fold). g NM, epithelioid subtype, Breslow depth 10 mm, Clark V, presence of microsatellites, absent TILs, and radial growth pattern (hematoxylin–eosin staining, magnification 30-fold). h NM, epithelioid subtype, proliferation index 48/1 mm2 (magnification 400-fold)
