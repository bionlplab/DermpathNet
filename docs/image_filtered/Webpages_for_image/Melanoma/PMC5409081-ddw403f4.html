A population-based analysis of germline BAP1 mutations in melanoma.

Histology of tumors from BAP1 probands. (A) Histopathological findings (x20) show an intradermal melanocytic lesion composed of pleomorphic melanocytes with abundant cytoplasm, many of which contain hyperchromatic nuclei. The dermo-epidermal junction (DEJ) is relatively spared. Multi-nucleated melanocytes (black arrow) and intranuclear pseudoinclusions (red arrows) are noted within this melanoma. (B) Histopathological findings (x10) show a melanoma with a predominantly spindled appearance. (C) There is an asymmetrical melanocytic proliferation composed of pleomorphic melanocytes, arranged in nests at the DEJ and extending into the dermis (x20). Some melanocytes have an epithelioid appearance with prominent nucleoli (thick black arrow), while others have hyperchromatic nuclei (red arrow). Bi-nucleated melanocytes are present (thin black arrow).
