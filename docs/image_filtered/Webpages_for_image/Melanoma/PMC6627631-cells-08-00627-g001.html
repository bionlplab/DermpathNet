Bone Marrow Involvement in Melanoma. Potentials for Detection of Disseminated Tumor Cells and Characterization of Their Subsets by Flow Cytometry.

Melanoma disseminated tumor cells in bone marrow. This figure presents a case of detection of skin melanoma cells in bone marrow punctate ((a) and (b), Ã—1000 magnification). Punctate bone marrow is poor. Normal lines of myelopoiesis are depressed. Cell complexes of a non-hemopoietic nature are determined. Additionally, there are scattered, separately lying tumor cells. They are represented by cells of a large size, and basophilic colored pigment granules of various sizes are visible in the cytoplasm. The morphological picture of the bone marrow is characteristic of metastatic lesions in melanoma.
