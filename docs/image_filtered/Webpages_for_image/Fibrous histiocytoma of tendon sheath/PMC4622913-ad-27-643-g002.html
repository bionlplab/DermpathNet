A Giant, Deep, Benign Fibrous Histiocytoma with a Palisading Pattern.

(A) Scanning view of the histopathological examination of the lesion showing an encapsulated mass located from the lower dermis through the subcutaneous tissue with a central cavity. The tumor was well circumscribed by a fibrous capsule, and no honeycomb pattern was detected in the marginal area. Hemangiopericytoma-like dilated vessels were seen in the periphery of the tumor (H&E, ×5). (B) Tumor cells arranged in a palisading pattern, similar to that of Verocay bodies, with a focal storiform pattern and partial myxoid change (H&E, ×40). (C) Spindle-shaped tumor cells with elongated nuclei and an ill-defined cytoplasm. Neither cellular atypia nor mitotic bodies were detected (H&E, ×400). Immunohistochemical staining showed (D) a strong positivity to CD34 (×40) and (E) negativity to S-100 (×40).

PMID: 26512193

Type: Benign, Category: Fibrohistiocytic
