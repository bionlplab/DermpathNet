Sebaceoma on the nose mimicking basal cell carcinoma: Pitfalls of dermoscopy and reflectance confocal microscopy.

Histopathological features. (A) A well‐circumscribed tumor in the dermis, surrounded by fibrous tissue and lymphocytes (hematoxylin‐eosin, low magnification). (B) Many basaloid cells with round or slightly elongated basophilic nuclei (hematoxylin‐eosin, ×100). (C) No peripheral palisading arrangement and retraction spaces between the tumor lobules and the stroma (hematoxylin‐eosin, ×200). (D) Scattered sebocytes with scalloped nuclei and vacuolated cytoplasm (hematoxylin‐eosin, ×400)

PMID: 36325590

Type: Benign, Category: Sebaceous
