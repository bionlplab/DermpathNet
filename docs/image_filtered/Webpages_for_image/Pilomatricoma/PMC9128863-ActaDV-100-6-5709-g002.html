Dermoscopic and Immunohistochemical Observations in Anetodermic Pilomatricoma: A Case Report.

Histopathological and immunohistochemical observation of anetodermic pilomatricoma. (a) A well-demarcated dermal tumour composed of peripheral basaloid cells, central eosinophilic shadow cells and transitional cells, with focal calcification in the area of shadow cells and vascular proliferation in the dermis (Haematoxylin and eosin stain; original magnification ×40). (b) Hyperpigmentation in the basal layer of epidermis and basaloid areas of tumour (Fontana-Masson stain; ×40). (c) Absence of elastic fibres in the supra-tumoural dermis (Elastic van Gieson stain; ×100). (d) Increased numbers of tryptase positive mast cells in the dermis (immunostaining; ×100). (e) CD31-positive vessels and lymphatics in the dermis (immunostaining; ×100). (f) D2-40 positive lymphatics in the dermis (immunostaining; ×100).

PMID: 32162670

Type: Benign, Category: Follicular
