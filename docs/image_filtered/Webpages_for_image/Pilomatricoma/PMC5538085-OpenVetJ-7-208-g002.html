Malignant pilomatricoma in a dog with local and distant metastases treated with chemotherapy and bisphosphonates.

Cytology of carcinoma metastases identified during the course of the disease: (A): left scapular mass (Wright Giemsa, 50x magnification); (B): liver (Wright Giemsa, 50x magnification); (C): left inguinal lymph node (Wright Giemsa, 50x magnification); (D): right orbital mass (Diff-Quick, 100x magnification). There are high numbers of atypical cells. They are seen in variably sized, apparently cohesive clusters. Occasional acinoid formations are found. The N:C ratio is high and anisokaryosis and anisocytosis are mild. Nuclei are medium sized, round and central to paracentral. Occasional hyperchromatic nuclei are seen. The chromatin is coarsely stippled and occasionally multiple small, round and variably prominent nucleoli are present. The cytoplasm is scant and lightly to moderately basophilic.

PMID: 28795016

Type: Benign, Category: Follicular
