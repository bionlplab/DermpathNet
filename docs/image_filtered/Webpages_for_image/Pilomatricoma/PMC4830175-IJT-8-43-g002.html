Giant Pilomatricoma of the arm: A diagnostic challenge for cytopathologist.

Fine needle aspiration cytology showing (a) fragment of basaloid cells, few ghost cells and area of necrosis with karyorrhectic debris. (H and E, ×100), (b) basaloid cells showing vesicular nucleus with prominent nucleoli and scanty cytoplasm (H and E, ×100), (c) giant cell (H and E, ×400) and (d) basaloid cells showing vesicular nucleus with prominent nucleoli and scanty cytoplasm (Leishman × 100)
