Tissue-remodelling M2 Macrophages Recruits Matrix Metallo-proteinase-9 for Cryotherapy-induced Fibrotic Resolution during Keloid Treatment.

Histologic analysis of keloids treated with cryotherapy. Hematoxylin and eosin (H&E) staining of (a, b) untreated keloid shows thick bundles of collagen fibers (scale bar =1 mm in lower magnification and 200 Î¼m in higher magnification). Cryotherapy-treated keloid (d, e) reveals replacement of abnormal collagen by fine collagen, as well as the infiltration of large round cells with elongated nuclei. Immunohistochemical staining (c, f) reveals that these infiltrated cells are CD68+ macrophages. Original maginification x25 and x100.
