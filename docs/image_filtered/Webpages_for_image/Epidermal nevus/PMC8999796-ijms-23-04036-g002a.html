Identification of Codon 146 KRAS Variants in Isolated Epidermal Nevus and Multiple Lesions in Oculoectodermal Syndrome: Confirmation of the Phenotypic Continuum of Mosaic RASopathies.-DermPath

Identification of Codon 146 KRAS Variants in Isolated Epidermal Nevus and Multiple Lesions in Oculoectodermal Syndrome: Confirmation of the Phenotypic Continuum of Mosaic RASopathies.

(A–D) Histopathology. (A) A skin biopsy from the slightly palpable Blaschkoid hyperpigmentation on the right arm in individual 1 shows features suggestive of epidermal nevus, including discrete acanthosis, hyperkeratosis and pigmentation of the basal layer (magnification ×100). (B) Similarly, epithelial hyperplasia and papillomatosis confirm the diagnosis of epidermal nevus on the chin of individual 2 (magnification ×400). (C,D) A skin biopsy taken in the focal region of alopecia in individual 1 shows the presence of fat in the dermis, tissue fibrosis and an anagen arrest of hair follicles, indicative of nevus psiloliparus (magnification ×100).

PMID: 35409398

Type: Benign, Category: Keratinocytic
