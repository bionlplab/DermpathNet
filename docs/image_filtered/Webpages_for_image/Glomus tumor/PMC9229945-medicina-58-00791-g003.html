Tracheal Glomus Tumor: A Case Report with CT Imaging Features.

The histopathological findings after surgery showing typical features of tracheal glomus tumor. (A) The tumor consisted of a proliferation of epithelioid cells and abundant vascular channels (hematoxylin and eosin [H&E], ×200); (B) tumor cells were abundant, single, round, and small-sized, with uniform eosinophilic or transparent cytoplasm and hyperchromatic nuclei and formed microvascular spaces (H&E, ×400); (C–F) immunohistochemistry showed tumor cells were diffusely positive for smooth muscle actin (SMA), focally positive for synaptophysin (Syn), but negative for CD56 and CD31 (c; SMA, ×100, d; Syn, ×100, e; CD56, ×100, f; CD31 ×100).

PMID: 35744054

Type: Benign, Category: Vascular
