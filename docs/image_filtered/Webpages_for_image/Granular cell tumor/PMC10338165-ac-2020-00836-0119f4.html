Experience of surgical treatment in a granular cell tumor in the qscending colon: a case report.

Microscopic findings. (A) The resected tumor was located in the submucosa, was covered with normal mucosa, and had a relatively well-circumscribed border (hematoxylin-eosin [H&E], ×1.25). (B) The tumor consisted of nests of polygonal cells, which had small round-tooval nuclei with no pleomorphism, as well as cytoplasm that was abundant, slightly granular, and brightly eosinophilic (H&E, ×200). Immunohistochemical staining revealed that the tumor cells were diffusely positive for CD68 (C) and S100 (D), but negative for normal epithelial cell markers.

PMID: 34228911

Type: Benign, Category: Neural or neuroendocrine
