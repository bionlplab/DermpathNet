Smooth Auricular Cutaneous Granular Cell Tumor Mimics Epidermoid Cyst.

The smooth cutaneous granular cell tumor is covered by apparently normal epidermis. (A) In gross appearance, it is a 0.8-cmsized, pinkish, mass with a well-formed capsule. In the subcutaneous fat layer, there are irregular, infiltrating borders and irregular entrapment of adipocytes by the granular cells (arrows) (hematoxylin–eosin stain, ×10). (B, C). Histopathologically, the tumor cells have abundant granular eosinophilic cytoplasm, round to oval nuclei. (C) Eosinophilic ovoid bodies are surrounded by a clear halo (arrowheads) (hematoxylin– eosin stain, ×100, ×200).

PMID: 31671932

Type: Benign, Category: Neural or neuroendocrine
