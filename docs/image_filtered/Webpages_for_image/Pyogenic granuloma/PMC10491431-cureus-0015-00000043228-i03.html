Halo Phenomenon in Lobular Capillary Hemangioma: A Case Report of a Pyogenic Granuloma With Surrounding Cutaneous Hypopigmentation and Review of Tumors With Halo Phenomenon.

Lobular capillary hemangioma with halo phenomenon: hematoxylin and eosin-stained central portion of lesion and epidermal collaretteLower (A and B) and higher (C and D) magnification views show hyperkeratosis (within the blue brackets), acanthosis (within the black brackets), and a collarette of the epithelium (within the black oval). The nodular lesion shows lobules of vascular proliferation (black arrows) within a fibrotic dermal stoma (blue circles) with minimal inflammation. The dermis, both deep and lateral to the vascular tumor, has solar elastosis (black triangles) (hematoxylin and eosin stain: A, x10; B, x10; C, x20; D, x20).
