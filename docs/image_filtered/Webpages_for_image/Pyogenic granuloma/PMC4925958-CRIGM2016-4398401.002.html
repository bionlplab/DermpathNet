Gastrointestinal Pyogenic Granuloma (Lobular Capillary Hemangioma): An Underrecognized Entity Causing Iron Deficiency Anemia.

Pyogenic granuloma of the stomach (Case 2). (a) Endoscopic appearance of the 12 mm gastric polypoid nodule with surface erosions. (b-c) Histologically, a capillary hemangioma is present and arranged in a lobular pattern and filled with clusters of small capillary vessels and neutrophils infiltrate ((b), ×100; (c), ×400). (d) Immunohistochemistry of CD31 highlighting the capillary proliferation (×400).

PMID: 27403353

Type: Benign, Category: Vascular
