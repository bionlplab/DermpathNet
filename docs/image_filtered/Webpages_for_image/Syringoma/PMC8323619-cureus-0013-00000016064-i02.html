Incidental Clear Cell Syringoma of the Scalp in a Patient With Lichen Planopilaris.

Scarring alopecia-associated clear cell syringomaHigher magnification demonstrates the clear cytoplasm of the syringoma cells (solid black arrowhead) and that some of the ducts show comma-like tails of epithelial cells (red arrow), making tadpole-like architectures (A). Perifollicular fibrosis (solid black arrow) with an accompanying lymphocytic infiltrate (open black arrowhead) and a syringoma duct (green arrow) are also present (B). (Hematoxylin and eosin: A, x200; B, x200).
