Syringomatous Adenoma of the Nipple on Screening Mammography: A Case Report.

A 42-year-old female patient with syringomatous adenoma of the left nipple.A. Grouped, coarse, heterogeneous microcalcifications are seen in the left nipple on the craniocaudal (left) and mediolateral oblique (right) magnification views.B. Ultrasonography shows microcalcifications in the left nipple (markers indicate the nipple).C. The lesion showed an infiltrative proliferation of the small glandular and tubular structures, often angulated features with a tear-drop shape or a comma shape (inset, arrows, × 400; H&E stain). The glands were composed of two cell layers–the inner epithelial cells (arrows) and the outer flattened myoepithelial cells (arrowheads) (× 40, H&E stain).H&E = hematoxylin and eosin, LMCC = left magnified craniocaudal, LMMLO = left magnified mediolateral oblique
