Cytopathologic features of an unusual case of multiple eccrine spiradenomas misdiagnosed as a malignant round cell tumor.

Histopathological findings. (a) Sharply defined cellular tumor nodules (H&E, ×100). (b) Tumor comprising round-to-oval cells surrounding “pink” hyaline bodies. Occasional duct formation noted (arrowhead) (H&E, ×200). (c) Dual population of small, dark, and relatively large, tumor cells with vesicular chromatin, around hyaline bodies. (H&E, ×400). (d) Tumor cells arranged in tubular and trabecular arrangement, including cells with pale chromatin toward lumens and relatively darker myoepithelial cells toward the periphery. Scattered lymphocytes noted (H&E, ×200)

PMID: 28182081

Type: Benign, Category: Adnexal
