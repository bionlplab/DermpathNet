Significance of Dermoscopy in Association with Clinical Features in Differentiation of Basal Cell Carcinoma and Benign Trichoblastic Tumours.

Two clinically similar tumours presenting as whitish plaque within the cheek area. (a,b) Desmoplastic trichoepithelioma; dermoscopy shows branched (arborizing) vessels (red arrow) over whitish background (Fotofinder Vexia; Camera Medicam 800 HD, ×20 magnification). (c,d) Morpheaform basal cell carcinoma; dermoscopy shows central ulceration (black arrow) and branched (arborizing) vessels (red arrow) over whitish background, starry milia-like cysts (white arrow) and Multiple Aggregated Yellow-White Globules (yellow arrow) (Fotofinder Vexia; Camera Medicam 800 HD, ×20 magnification).

PMID: 36010957

Type: Benign, Category: Follicular
