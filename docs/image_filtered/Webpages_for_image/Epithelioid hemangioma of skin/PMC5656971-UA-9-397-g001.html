Epithelioid hemangioma of penis mimicking malignancy: A rare case.

(a) Photograph of the lesion noted at the tip of the meatus, measuring approximately 3 cm × 2 cm. (b) Low-power view of the lesion showing an unencapsulated subcutaneous vascular lesion (H and E, ×10). (c) Section showing plump endothelial cells with abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm (H and E, ×20). (d) Image showing epithelioid endothelial cells with moderate eosinophilic cytoplasm along with scattered lymphocytes and eosinophils (marked with black arrow) (H and E, ×40). (e and f) Photomicrograph showing positive immunohistochemistry for CD31 (in epithelioid endothelial cells) and smooth muscle antigen (in myopericyte) (immunohistochemistry, ×40)

PMID: 29118548

Type: Benign, Category: Vascular
