Epithelioid hemangioma (ALHE) on the tongue of an infant treated with oral corticosteroids: A case report and review of the literature.

The top left picture presents an extensive ulcerated lesion on the tongue before treatment (The ulcerated area was present on the anterior third of the dorsal surface of his tongue with rather sharp edges and focal extensions to the ventral surface without active hemorrhage) and top right picture presents decrease in size after treatment with prednisolone. (a) Tongue muscle with overlying epithelioid hemangioma (Hematoxylin and Eosin (H&E), original magnification ×4). (b) Epithelioid vessels (in ALHE blood vessels are lined by plump epithelioid endothelial cells) with intracytoplasmic lumen containing Red Blood Cells (RBCs) (arrow) and frequent eosinophilic infiltration. (c) Vessels with hobnail endothelium (H&E, original magnification ×10)

PMID: 37636169
