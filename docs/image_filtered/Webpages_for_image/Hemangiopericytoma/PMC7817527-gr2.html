Central nervous system solitary fibrous tumour/hemangiopericytoma presenting as nausea, vomiting and hepatic dysfunction after the first trimester of pregnancy: A case report.

Haematoxylin and eosin (H&E) staining (original magnification 20×) reveals a highly cellular tumour with a typical “staghorn” vascular channel and the proliferation of oval- to spindle-shaped cells around the vessels, revealing brisk mitotic activity (A). Neoplastic cells are immunoreactive for STAT6(original magnification 20×) (B). Neoplastic cells are diffusely immunoreactive for CD34 (original magnification 20×) (C). Neoplastic cells are diffusely immunoreactive for vimentin (original magnification 20×) (D). EMA negativity in tumour cells (E). T S-100 negativity in tumour cells (F).

PMID: 33511039

Type: Benign, Category: Vascular
