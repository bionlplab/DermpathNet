Genetic rearrangements, hotspot mutations, and microRNA expression in the progression of metastatic adenoid cystic carcinoma of the salivary gland.

Histological features of primary and metastatic salivary gland adenoid cystic carcinoma(A) Adenoid cystic carcinoma of the sinonasal minor salivary glands with tubular and cribriform areas and accumulation of basophilic basal membrane material in pseudoluminae (case 6) (hematoxylin & eosin, H&E). (B) Adenoid cystic carcinoma of the submandibular gland from case 1 with solid aggregates of basaloid tumor cells. Nuclei are large and mitoses are frequent (H&E). (C) Pulmonary metastasis of a well-differentiated tubulocribriform adenoid cystic carcinoma from the primary tumor in (A). Normal lung parenchyma is seen to the right (asterisk) (H&E). (D) Hepatic metastasis showing a solid adenoid cystic carcinoma. This specimen is from the same patient as in (A and C). Normal liver parenchyma is seen to the right (asterisk) (H&E). Scale bar = 100 Î¼m.
