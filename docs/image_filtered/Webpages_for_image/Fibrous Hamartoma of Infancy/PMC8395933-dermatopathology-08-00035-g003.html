Update on Superficial Spindle Cell Mesenchymal Tumors in Children.

Fibrous hamartoma of infancy (FHI). (A) Typical clinical presentation as an axillary mass (courtesy of the Department of Maxillofacial Surgery, Necker-Enfants Malades hospital, Paris, France). (B) Low-power view of the lesion showing location in the subcutis and extension of the spindle-cell component into the dermis (HE ×50). (C) Presence of the three typical components (mature fibrous tissue, mature adipose tissue, immature mesenchymal tissue) (HE ×100). (D) In some cases, the immature mesenchymal tissue is not visible (HE ×100). (E) Peri-eccrine extension of the lesion (HES ×100). (F) Hyalinized zone reminiscent of giant cell fibroblastoma (HES ×100).

PMID: 34449590

Type: Benign, Category: Fibrohistiocytic
