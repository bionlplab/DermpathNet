Dedifferentiated liposarcoma of the oral floor: A case study and literature review of 50 cases of head and neck neoplasm.

Histological examination of the specimen. (A) Hematoxylin and eosin staining revealed that the specimen comprised two distinct areas, *indicates the well-differentiated area and **indicates the dedifferentiated area. Magnification, ×100. (B) In the dedifferentiated area, bizarre multinucleate giant cells were occasionally observed. Magnification, ×200. (C) In the well-differentiated area, adipocytic proliferation with hyperchromatic stromal cells was observed. Magnification, ×200.
