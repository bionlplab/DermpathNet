Primary intrathoracic liposarcomas: A clinicopathologic and molecular study of 43 cases in one of the largest medical centers of China.

The histologic features of myxoid pleomorphic liposarcoma and corresponding immunohistochemical and genetic results. The tumor exhibited features of pleomorphic liposarcoma with myxoid matrix, and multivacuolated lipoblasts were found (A H&E; magnification: 400×) (case 42). Myxoid liposarcoma-like areas were also identified within the tumor, displaying a well-developed plexiform vasculature pattern (B H&E; magnification: 400×). The tumor cells (case 42) showed positivity for p53 immunostaining (C magnification: 400×). FISH analysis revealed negativity for MDM2 amplification (D) or DDIT3 rearrangement (E). Sanger sequencing results showed TP53 mutations in both tumors (F).
